📊 Comparatives & Superlatives

How to say "more," "less," "most," and "least" in Spanish
💡 In English we say "taller" or "more tall." In Spanish, you always use a formula instead of changing the word ending!
TypeFormulaExampleMeans
Comparative (+) más + adj + que más alto que taller than
Comparative (−) menos + adj + que menos frío que less cold than
Equal tan + adj + como tan bonito como as pretty as
Superlative (most) el/la más + adj el más caliente the hottest
Superlative (least) el/la menos + adj la menos fría the least cold
Example with seasons 🍂
El verano es más caliente que el invierno.
Summer is hotter than winter.
Superlative example ☀️
El verano es la estación más caliente.
Summer is the hottest season.
Equal comparison 🌸
La primavera es tan bonita como el otoño.
Spring is as pretty as fall.
💡 Watch out! bueno (good) and malo (bad) have irregular comparatives: mejor (better) and peor (worse) — just like "good → better" in English!
